The Civil Rights movement arrives in Phoenix.

1954

Schools are legally desegregated in 1954, and George Washington Carver High School closes. It will later reopen as the George Washington Carver Museum and Cultural Center.

More about this project's work with George Washington Carver Museum and Cultural Center.

1955

Segregation in city housing is outlawed in 1955 (De Potter Lykes in Johnson Jr., G.W. 1993 p 228).

1959

Phoenix begins rapidly expanding its boundaries. In 1959, minority neighborhoods south of downtown finally become an official part of the City of Phoenix.

Citations:

De Potter Lykes, A. (1993). Phoenix Women in the Development of Public Policy: Territorial Beginnings. In G. Wesley Johnson, Jr. (Eds.), Phoenix in the Twentieth Century: Essays in Community History. pp. 221.
