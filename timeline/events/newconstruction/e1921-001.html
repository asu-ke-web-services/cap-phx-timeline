In 1921, Phoenix has 35 miles of paved streets, 64 miles of sidewalks, 125 miles of unpaved streets, and 21 miles of street car lines.

Anglo neighborhoods see the expansion of water and sewage infrastructure. Historic South Phoenix remains outside of the boundaries of the city and residents there receive no such services. For more see politics

New technologies like elevators, air cooling systems, and new building materials bring high-rise development to downtown Phoenix (Wilson, Brownell, and Goldfield quoted in Kottlanger 1983).

The canal along Van Buren Street between Central and 3rd avenues is closed to keep cars and pedestrians safe. Workers begin to line other canals with concrete (Kotlanger 1983, 32).

Landfills open along the Salt River (Bolin et al. 2005, p. 162 citing Mawn 1979).

The city forms a municipal airport.
