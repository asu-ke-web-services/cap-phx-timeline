The city’s sewer system begins operation in 1911. Some South Phoenix residents will not have access to sewage and waste services for three decades.

The Center Street Bridge crosses the Salt River to connect communities on either side of the banks (De Potter Lykes in Johnson Jr., G.W. 1993 p 222).

The original La Imaculada Concepción de Santa Maria is replaced. Spanish-speaking parishioners are only allowed to worship in the basement of the new church (Oberle and Arreol, 2008,176).

By now, Phoenix has several miles of sidewalk and 100 city blocks of pavement. The city is still growing. By 1916, it has almost 25,000 residents (Horton 1941, 30-32).

The Phoenix Brickyard is constructed in 1917 in Central City South. It will still be operating in 2012
