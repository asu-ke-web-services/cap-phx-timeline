The 1880s is a busy decade for Phoenix. The city has grown to more than 2,450 people. In 1882 there is a building boom (Horton 1941, 21).

Building homes and businesses with brick is popular. The use of adobe declines (Oberle and Arreola 2008, 175 citing Montvalo 2006).

Mexican merchants donate land to build La Imaculada Concepción de Santa Maria on East Monroe between Pima and Pinal streets (Obere and Arreoa 2008, 176). These are now 3rd and 4th streets.

The Southern Pacific Railroad is built in 1887. It runs east to west, forming what will become a clear line between North and South Phoenix. This spurs industrial development in South Phoenix (Luckingham 1994; Bolin et al. 2005).
