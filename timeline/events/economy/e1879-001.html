1879

Prescott's Bank of Arizona opens a Phoenix branch.

See: De Potter Lykes, A. (1993). Phoenix Women in the Development of Public Policy: Territorial Beginnings. In G. Wesley Johnson, Jr. (Eds.), Phoenix in the Twentieth Century: Essays in Community History. pp. 221.

1883

Valley Bank organized with $50,000 in capital.

See: Horton, A. G. (1941). An Economic, Political and Social Survey of Phoenix and the Valley of the Sun. Tempe, AZ: Southside Progress.
