Phoenix exhausts its groundwater pumping capacities in the 1920s. The city moves water from the Verde River to solve the crisis (Kottlanger,1983).

The canal along Van Buren Street between Central and 3rd avenues is closed to keep cars and pedestrians safe. Workers begin to line other canals with concrete.

Citations:

Kotlanger, M. 1983. Phoenix, Arizona: 1920-1940. Doctoral Dissertation. Arizona State University, Tempe, AZ.
